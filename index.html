The Universal Problem

Every human being — religious or not —
has sinned and fallen short of God.

Sin is not merely murder or theft. Sin is any thought, word, or action that falls short of God's perfect standard — pride, jealousy, lies, selfishness, hatred. The honest truth is: every person who has ever lived has sinned. Every culture. Every religion. No exceptions.

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God." — Romans 3:23
Every culture
Every religion
Every race
Every generation
Every person

And sin carries a consequence: "The wages of sin is death" — not only physical death, but eternal separation from God. This is the problem Good Friday answers. That's exactly why Jesus came.

The Answer

What is Good Friday?

Good Friday is the day Christians solemnly remember the crucifixion of Jesus Christ — the Son of God — who willingly gave His life on a Roman cross outside Jerusalem around 30 AD.

Why call a day of death "Good"? Because what looked like history's greatest tragedy was actually history's greatest act of love. Jesus was not a victim. He walked to that cross knowingly, willingly, and purposefully.

He took the punishment we deserved. He paid the debt we could never pay. In doing so, He opened the door to eternal life for every person who would believe.

"He was pierced for our transgressions, He was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was upon Him, and by His wounds we are healed." — Isaiah 53:5 (written 700 years before Jesus)

This is documented history — a real man, a real cross, a real death — witnessed by hundreds and recorded by historians of that era. Three days later, the tomb was empty.

No religion, background, or past disqualifies you. The invitation is open to every human being on earth.

His Final Hours

The Journey to Calvary

From the garden to the grave — every step was taken for you.

I

The Garden of Gethsemane

Jesus knelt alone in agony, sweating drops like blood, and prayed: "Father, if You are willing, take this cup from Me; yet not My will, but Yours be done." He chose the cross before a single nail was driven. — Luke 22:42–44

II

Betrayal & Trial

Judas betrayed Him with a kiss for thirty pieces of silver. Pilate declared, "I find no basis for a charge against this man," yet condemned Him to death to satisfy the crowd. The innocent stood condemned so the guilty could go free. — Luke 23:4; John 18–19

III

Flogging & Crown of Thorns

Soldiers flogged Him severely, twisted together a crown of thorns and pressed it onto His head, and mocked Him: "Hail, king of the Jews!" He endured every blow in silence. — Matthew 27:27–30; John 19:1–3

IV

The Crucifixion

At nine in the morning they nailed Him to the cross between two criminals. A sign was placed above His head: "JESUS OF NAZARETH, THE KING OF THE JEWS." Even then He prayed: "Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing." — Mark 15:25; Luke 23:34; John 19:19

V

"It Is Finished"

At three in the afternoon, darkness covered the land, the earth shook, and the temple curtain was torn in two from top to bottom. Jesus cried: "It is finished." And He bowed His head and gave up His spirit. The debt of every human sin — paid in full. — John 19:30; Matthew 27:51

Why It Matters To You

The Problem, The Answer, The Gift

⚖️

The Problem: Sin

Every human has sinned and is separated from God. No amount of good works can erase this debt. The consequence is spiritual death — eternal separation from the God who made us and loves us.

"The wages of sin is death." Romans 6:23a
✝️

The Answer: The Cross

God did not leave us without hope. He sent His own Son — sinless and perfect — to bear our punishment. Jesus died the death we deserved and rose on the third day, defeating sin and death forever.

"While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." Romans 5:8
🕊️

The Gift: New Life

This is not religion — it is relationship. By believing and turning to Jesus, you receive full forgiveness, peace with God, and eternal life. The gift is completely free. It requires only faith.

"The gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord." Romans 6:23b
