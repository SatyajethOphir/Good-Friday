The Universal Problem

Every person has sinned
and fallen short of God

Sin is not merely murder or theft. Sin is any thought, word, or action that falls short of God's perfect standard — pride, jealousy, lies, selfishness, hatred.

The honest truth is: every person who has ever lived has sinned. Every culture. Every religion. No exceptions.

"For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God."

— Romans 3:23

Every Culture

Across all civilisations and belief systems, the reality of human moral failure is universal and undeniable.

Every Religion

Every major faith acknowledges that humans fall short of the divine standard — only in Christ is full atonement offered.

The Consequence

"The wages of sin is death" — not only physical death, but eternal separation from the God who loves us. This is the problem Good Friday answers.

The Hope

God did not leave us without an answer. That's exactly why Jesus came. The cross is God's answer to humanity's greatest problem.

The Answer

What is Good Friday?

Why call a day of death "Good"? Because what looked like history's greatest tragedy was actually history's greatest act of love.

The Problem: Sin

Every human is separated from God. No amount of good works can erase this debt. The consequence is spiritual death — eternal separation from the God who made us.

"The wages of sin is death." Romans 6:23a

The Answer: The Cross

God sent His own Son — sinless and perfect — to bear our punishment. Jesus died the death we deserved and rose on the third day, defeating sin and death forever.

"While we were still sinners, Christ died for us." Romans 5:8

The Journey to Calvary

From the garden to the grave — every step was taken for you.

The Garden of Gethsemane

Jesus knelt alone in agony, sweating drops like blood: "Father, not My will, but Yours be done." He chose the cross before a single nail was driven. — Luke 22:42–44

Betrayal & Trial

Judas betrayed Him for thirty pieces of silver. Pilate declared "I find no fault in this man," yet condemned Him to death. The innocent condemned so the guilty could go free. — Luke 23:4

Flogging & Crown of Thorns

Soldiers flogged Him severely, pressed a crown of thorns onto His head and mocked: "Hail, King of the Jews!" He endured every blow in silence. — Matthew 27:29

The Crucifixion

At nine in the morning they nailed Him to the cross. Even then He prayed: "Father, forgive them, for they know not what they do." — Luke 23:34; John 19:19

"It Is Finished"

Darkness covered the land. The temple curtain tore in two. Jesus cried: "It is finished." The debt of every human sin — paid in full. — John 19:30
